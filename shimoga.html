Shivamogga

Jog Falls

Jog Falls is created by the Sharavathi River dropping 253 m,making it the second-highest plunge waterfall in India

Sakrebyle Elephant camp

The main attraction of the camp is the interaction session allowed between the visitors and the elephants.

Kodachadri

Mountain peak with dense forests, 78km from Shimoga, it is 13th highest peak of Karnataka.

Koodli

Koodli is 16 km from Shivamogga, a place where rivers Tunga and Bhadra flow together.

Agumbe

Called "The Cherrapunji of the South", On a fine evening, the sunset can be seen over the Arabian Sea.

Gajanur Dam

Located 12 km from Shimoga en-route Thirthahalli, one can witness a perfect picnic spot in a way
of Tunga Dam built over River Tunga.

Gudavi Bird Sanctuary

A paradise for bird watchers. The sanctuary is located in the town of Sorab near Shimoga on the banks of Gudvi Lake.

Barkana Falls

Buzzing with life, the canopy of vegetation gets dense enough for any traveler to lose orientation.

Hidlumane Falls

Located in the dense Mookambika forests, It is a series of 6 - 7 mesmerising waterfalls in the district of Shimoga.

Jogigundi Falls

Tucked away from the prying eyes of the
world,the falls are situated in the midst of
the dense forests of Western Ghats.

Kuppali

It is famous for being the birthplace and childhood home of the renowned Kannada playwright and poet Kuvempu.

Linganamakki Dam

It has a length of 2.74 kilometres stretching across the Sharavathi river. It is located about 6 km from Jog Falls

Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary

It is a small picturesque island located in the River Tunga, 32 km from Shimoga town on the way to Thirthahalli.

Onake Abbi Falls

Towering waterfall tumbling over a high rock cliff, reachable by a lush jungle path.

Tavarekoppa Lion and Tiger Safari

Located 10 km from Shimoga, a thick cover of forest is situated, inhabiting tigers and lions moving freely in its deep parts.

Shivagange Falls

Located near Sirsi, the water cascading down cliffs amidst dense woods and plenteous vegetation is a
popular trekking spot.

The name of the city is derived from the term "shivmoga". A version of etymology is due to the story that Lord Shiva drank the Tunga river water using "Mogge", hence the name Shiva-mogga".
Another version of etymology is that the name is derived from the term "Sihi-Mogge", meaning "sweet pot".
The district formed the southern tip of the Emperor Ashoka's Mauryan Empire in the third century BC.It was ruled during later centuries by the Kadambas (4th century), Chalukyas (6th century), Ganges, Rashtrakutas (8th century), Hoysalas (11th century), and the Vijayanagara rulers (15th century).
The city got an independent identity under the Keladi Nayakas' rule during the 16th century.From the late 17th century,
the city had been a part of the Kingdom of Mysore until the independence of India in 1947, when the
Mysore state merged into the Republic of India.
It has been home to many scenic view points, Waterfalls, Heritage sites and many more tourist spots.
